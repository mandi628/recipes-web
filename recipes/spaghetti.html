Simple Spaghetti

Description

This simple spaghetti recipe will keep your family happy for years. Make the sauce ahead, or double it and freeze the leftovers for a quick dinner night later. Monday night is always spaghetti night in our house.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (about 2 teaspoons)
  • 1 28-oz. can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 pound spaghetti
  • salt
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet on medium or medium high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until transclucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  4. While the onions are cooking, put a large pot of salted water on to boil for the pasta.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes to the cooked onions and garlic.
  6. Heat just until simmering.
  7. Once the pasta water has come to a rolling boil, add the spaghetti.
  8. Once the water has returned to boiling, cook, uncovered, for 10-12 minutes.
  9. Remove pasta pot from heat, and drain pasta before placing in a large serving bowl.
  10. Toss pasta with sauce and serve immediately.
  11. Garnish with Parmesan cheese.

Variations

Spaghetti sauce variations are as common as there are cooks. Here are some ideas to change it up from time to time.

  • Add Italian sausage (mild or hot). Squeeze it from the casing and brown it.
  • Add bulk pork sausage (the breakfast kind).
  • Add ground beef.
  • Add other vegtables, chopped finely: green peppers, celery, shredded carrot.
  • Try different herbal blends: Italian seasoning, oregano, basil, thyme, marjoram, etc.
